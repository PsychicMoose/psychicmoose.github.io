Get to Know Me

Hey, I’m Daniel—a builder and creative engineer focused on AI, automation, and human systems. I recently graduated cum laude from Northeastern University with a degree in Computer Engineering, and I’m now pursuing my master’s in Cyber-Physical Systems while launching my own automation business.

I grew up in Fort Lee, New Jersey, the kind of kid who took apart everything just to see how it worked. That curiosity led me to the Academy for Engineering and Design Technology at Bergen County Academies, where I dove deep into 3D modeling, programming, and circuit design. It’s also where I realized: breaking things is fun—but building things is better.

Since then, I’ve worked in both hardware and software roles at Sparx Hockey and Stemwave, completed projects ranging from stereo vision wearables to full-stack automation flows, and developed a bias for building systems that solve real problems and ship fast. Right now, I’m helping small businesses streamline client operations using n8n, Notion, and GPT-4—and I’m mapping out an AI-powered voice assistant wearable: minimalist, screenless, designed to support behavior and reflection rather than distraction.

Outside of work, I tinker with electronics, sketch out new ideas, journal, and write. I’m drawn to stories with depth, strangeness, and meaning—Murakami, BoJack Horseman, Vonnegut, Community. I also led NUTV’s entertainment department, helping creators take their wildest ideas from pitch to production.

Thanks for stopping by, feel free check out my projects below or reach out if you want to build something real.
